## Install

**Already on v0.4.0? You don't need this page.** Your launcher's **Update &
Restart** button downloads this release with a live progress readout and
installs it by itself — silently on Windows; on Linux by swapping the new
AppImage over the same file, so a Steam shortcut pointing at it keeps working —
then relaunches the game. This is the first release delivered through that
path; if anything goes wrong it falls back to opening this page.

**Windows (fresh install)** — download `HollowWake-Setup-0.5.0.exe` below and
run it. It is a normal game installer: pick a folder (defaults to a per-user
install, no admin rights needed) and it puts a **Hollow Wake** shortcut on the
desktop and in the Start Menu, plus an uninstaller entry in *Apps & features*.
Windows SmartScreen may warn that the publisher is unknown — the build is
unsigned — choose **More info → Run anyway**.

**Linux / Steam Deck (fresh install)** — download
`HollowWake-0.5.0-x86_64.AppImage`, mark it executable (`chmod +x`), and run
it. On the Deck, add it as a non-Steam game from Desktop Mode (or run the
install one-liner from STEAM.md — it fetches the latest release). Launching
from Steam boots straight into the game.

Saves live in your per-user data folder (Windows: `%APPDATA%/Hollow Wake`), so
installing, updating, or uninstalling the app never touches them.

## Highlights since v0.4.0 (65 commits in 2 days)

**The game opens like a game now.** A new account begins with The Last Mile —
a one-time playable prologue: a caravan drill, goblin waves, and Ghorvane the
Hordefather, whose Warhorn you can break to silence the reckoning it musters —
before waking at a bedside in Lastlight. It's a scene, not a run: no loot, no
mid-scene saves, and quitting out re-offers it on your next New Game.

**The shop learned scarcity, patronage, and secrecy.** Counters restock on a
five-minute beat you can plan around (and shorten with Rush Order rungs), and
every shelf is dealt from the world's own seed — re-entering town or reloading
meets the same wares, and sold-out stays sold-out until the beat turns. Every
counter got two faces: a proper wares grid, and a gems case that stands sealed
until you earn the Gem Counter. Reserve wares in unlocked slots, or place a
Standing Order for a gem you've seen drop — it resolves at the shelf's true
odds even while you're away adventuring. The Vault itself became tabbed
shelves that keep the world's secrets: content you haven't met simply isn't
mentioned, and the store grows as the world introduces itself. And roofed
counters now serve only under their own roof — no more shop panels
auto-opening as you cross the square.

**Your gear is worn on a body.** The equipment panel is a figure now — helmet
to boots down the spine, rings and gloves on the flanks, a faint silhouette
standing behind the seats, and every empty seat teaching what goes where with
a ghost glyph. All on one screen, with every drag, drop, and tooltip behavior
exactly as before.

**Gear can carry your supports.** New "of the Nth Finger" suffixes (and The
Rote Hand unique) graft a support gem onto a hotbar seat itself — you aim the
grant by choosing what you bind there. And loadout surgery became a camp
habit: unlearning skills and re-socketing supports wants cold blades and no
live hostiles nearby — while in a sanctuary, the workshop is always free.

**The land got its vertical truth.** Real elevation under everything: mountain
ranges stand on genuine ridge spines, land dips toward every shore, and rivers
now rise at springs and trace true downhill courses to their mouths, crossing
whole countries on the way — on-river zones carve flowing riverland ground in
the local dress, and the world map draws the same rivers the world mints.

**The heavens finished their crown.** The Cathedral of the Highest — whole
cruciform basilicas raised over the cloudsea, with a walkable glass-floor
promenade — anchors the Civitas, the true angelic city. Its call-and-response
Liturgy skill lane is taught by its own doors, and the Galestream lays a
wind-river throughline across the Aetherial's bastion country.

**New countries, new crowds.** The starter wood grew into the Grove Country —
staged rim to heart, with dusk-to-dawn firefly tides drifting on a living
light layer. The passive tree was rescued and reshaped into 442 nodes with
real either-or choice deals. The throng playstyle blew its acquisition wide
open — claimable pockets, timed motes, kill triggers, a hit-fed gauge for
add-less bosses, brood trickles, all graftable by gems — and its latching kin
learned to gnaw on what they ride and to burrow inside it. The Pit stands as a
deliberate XP arena that mints no gear at all.

**A harbor is earned.** Mainland ports rebuilt as a pair: a besieged walled
hold that contains the door to its offshore port — break the siege and the
causeway opens, with services growing on the quay as the town prospers. No
land road auto-forges across ocean anymore; crossing a sea is a voyage.
Mercenaries got their market law too: muster-roll locks, wilds commissions,
and a recruiter standing in Lastlight.

**Under the hood, honesty.** The support-gem audit arc drove the no-op ledger
from ~18,500 open rows to ~5,800 — gems that silently did nothing now work,
honestly refuse the socket until their mechanism exists, or gained genuinely
new machinery (charge magazines, empower banks, hit-to-wound bargains,
aftermath zones; cascade and pulse gems now deepen a skill's native lanes
instead of overwriting them). Field bugs closed: dying while slowed no longer
wakes you slow forever, your summoned court arrives with you through side
zones, paths stopped paving through walls, and couch co-op pad claims survive
the join ceremony.
