## Install

**Windows** — download `HollowWake-Setup-0.2.0.exe` below and run it. It is a
normal game installer: pick a folder (defaults to a per-user install, no admin
rights needed) and it puts a **Hollow Wake** shortcut on the desktop and in the
Start Menu, plus an uninstaller entry in *Apps & features*. Windows SmartScreen
may warn that the publisher is unknown — the build is unsigned — choose
**More info → Run anyway**.

**Linux / Steam Deck** — download `HollowWake-0.2.0-x86_64.AppImage`, mark it
executable (`chmod +x`), and run it. On the Deck, add it as a non-Steam game
from Desktop Mode.

Saves live in your per-user data folder (Windows: `%APPDATA%/Hollow Wake`), so
installing, updating, or uninstalling the app never touches them. The launcher
checks GitHub on start and tells you when a newer release exists — its Update
button opens the download page.

## Highlights since v0.1.0 (93 commits in 3 days)

**The graphics overhaul, rounds 1–11.** The whole visual fabric landed in this
window: a materials system with baked, shaded bodies; terrain chunks with
mottle, wall bevels and contact shadows; doodads as a painter registry; the
dynamic light layer (night, emissives, combat glow, lights that pool at
walls); weather particles, shaded roofs, living liquids and mirrored ice; wind
you can shelter from (it ends at the eaves); forests you walk under and fog
you vanish in; real top-down monster portraits composed from a part grammar —
including plural-hitbox composites like the Marsh Leviathan; structure floors,
masonry walls, a rock grammar with a rare monolith tail; positional palettes
(coastlines fade, clearings glow); the taiga with snow that sticks where it's
cold, under an aurora; and roads that finally read as roads.

**The world filled with lives.** Roughly sixty new monsters across nine
families — cap-folk, cavern dwellers, treants, beastkin, fleshspawn, rookeries,
apparitions and the Night Court, wolves, and vermin with egg-filled lairs —
plus the small lives that make a zone feel inhabited. New habitat seams: siege
garrisons, monsters that dive into refuges, wildlife near water, ambushers in
the ground itself, and the Haunting, a seventeenth world-event package.

**The Legion mustered.** Ten new demons with their own skills and supports —
and PRESENCE: leveled-list spawn envelopes on every monster table, so rosters
shift gradually as you descend instead of swapping wholesale.

**Combat got honest.** Casting locks your body to the aim you committed;
Foresight telegraphs enemy melee swings, breath cones and hostile novas; poise
is a true break-bar with a visible re-arm; energy-shield recharge is an
interruptible stream; and enemy-conjured summons pay no bounty — kill the
summoner, not the stream.

**Skills learned new tricks.** The cadence lever — skills that accelerate or
taper on every beat (Accelerando / Ritardando, the Carillon bell family, the
Fumarole: ground that throws true projectiles) — plus five composable levers
(soak-in fume domains, converging sweeps, worn ground, follow-through casts,
channel drag) feeding six new skill families: fumes, walls of meat, harvest
sweeps, grasping tendrils, verdant vines, and the Undertow.

**Minions and their gems, reworked.** Support gems now forward to minions
directly — a gem serves exactly the lanes it fits — with Resonance boarding
and crews that tax their host.

**Playability.** Controller-grade aiming (thematic cursor, pad reticle, soft
sticky aim assist, snapback filtering), dwell-to-compare tooltips, equipment
slot highlights, live keybind hints, and dent-gated monster bars — an
untouched monster shows no bar at all.
