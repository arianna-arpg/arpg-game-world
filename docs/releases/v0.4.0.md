## Install

**Windows** — download `HollowWake-Setup-0.4.0.exe` below and run it. It is a
normal game installer: pick a folder (defaults to a per-user install, no admin
rights needed) and it puts a **Hollow Wake** shortcut on the desktop and in the
Start Menu, plus an uninstaller entry in *Apps & features*. Windows SmartScreen
may warn that the publisher is unknown — the build is unsigned — choose
**More info → Run anyway**.

**Linux / Steam Deck** — download `HollowWake-0.4.0-x86_64.AppImage`, mark it
executable (`chmod +x`), and run it. On the Deck, add it as a non-Steam game
from Desktop Mode (or run the install one-liner from STEAM.md — it fetches the
latest release). Launching from Steam boots straight into the game.

Saves live in your per-user data folder (Windows: `%APPDATA%/Hollow Wake`), so
installing, updating, or uninstalling the app never touches them.

**This install updates itself from now on.** When a newer release exists, the
launcher's **Update & Restart** button downloads it with a live progress
readout and installs it in place — silently on Windows; on Linux by swapping
the new AppImage over the same file, so a Steam shortcut pointing at it keeps
working — then relaunches the game. No browser, no manual download. (Coming
from v0.3.0, this one last install is by hand — the old launcher only knows
how to open this page.)

## Highlights since v0.3.0 (88 commits in 3 days)

**Couch co-op arrived.** Plug in a second controller and a second hero joins
from the pause menu — Diablo-couch style on one screen. The view stretches to
hold you both up to a hard cap, then holds its line: at the frame's edge you
run in place until your partner turns back. The HUD splits to your flanks,
inventories and sheets dock to whichever side opened them over the one shared
map, and shops, benches and waystones answer whichever player is standing at
them. A mortal run seats a fresh disposable hero; an immortal run seats a
second sworn vessel from the account roster — it saves to its own slot and
pays its own covenant on a wipe. Solo play is byte-identical by construction.
Built for exactly the Deck-in-a-dock test: two pads, one screen.

**The underworld got its ferry.** The River of Souls: an inland sea where the
soul-water is the ground and the Pale Ferry is the moving landmass — board it
at a landing, ride its dealt route, and step off at shores that stay sealed
forever after (no door ever accretes that wasn't notarized). Its many landing
mouths sit on the world chart as berths of the one river, and the wrecked
boardwalks between islets are walked at your own risk.

**The map thinks ahead of you now.** Every ocean is known whole the moment
you touch its water — named, classed pond-to-ocean, and ringed with explicit
port spots. Mainland ports stand as besieged walled quay-towns: relieve the
siege at the quay ward wave by wave and the town opens, grows services with
every defense (harbormaster, chandler, a mercenary captain), and can fall and
be rebuilt. A veiled halo of fully-minted world is charted ahead of your walk,
omens age wider so nothing stays lost, harbor boards sell charts and hearsay —
and a zone's name is earned by walking its ground; everything else is honestly
Uncharted.

**Spent ground runs quick again.** The Quickening: a walked, outgrown zone
surges to your level for one window — re-minted, denser, richer, anchored by a
champion surge echo under quickened air — then reverts exactly. The slayer
support lane (overmatch, giantsbane, regicide, limbreaver) folds in beside it.

**You can leave your body.** Possession enters a weakened enemy and drives its
kit while its kin still read you as one of their own — until the first blow.
Shapeshifting mints the form at your level. And Mimicry opens the blue-mage
lane: bestiary-gated capture of enemy arts into your own bar.

**Zones grew stories.** True verticality inside one zone: the sewers run under
the civic rings, mountains carry switchback terrace summits with cavern
networks inside their own rock, the Ascent climbs by stories, and the Gale
family lays wind-made roads between them.

**New countries, and crowds to fill them.** The Seraph City citadel in the
High Heavens; the bug-high Garden Country, an enormous tended plot walked at
seed scale where a flower stalk is a tower; the farmland-and-metropolis
settled belt whose folds genuinely stray and get called home by the bell. The
lite tier pours hundreds of bodies for the price of none — wade-through vermin
tides, self-regrowing colonies, and a TRAMPLE verb underfoot — while
multi-part composite monsters (crack the wall, silence the blessing, choose
the head) walk the ordinary spawn tables below boss tier.

**Combat grew hands.** The grab fabric: carry, drag, pin, swallow, throw —
with a struggle ladder, ally severs, and grip-kin tutors. Mass and authority
decide who moves whom: the heavy shove harder, arrested momentum wounds
against walls, and the bowling lane slams bodies through bodies. The combo
grammar makes cast-cadence a build axis the cadenced kin drum too; the throng
is the swarm you gather and pour; trapworks arm the ruins with saw halls,
boulder runs and false floors; and the confusion family makes control itself a
symmetric hit surface.

**Thirty-six classes, found before bought.** Every point of the nine-pointed
star anchors four classes, each with a vocation line; the discovery web makes
finding them the unlock; the roster reaches its twelfth slot; and the
Harborwarden's company lane hires from the holds you saved.

**Polish and speed.** The first press always answers (panels, menus, map);
the settings screen became a proper Options book with a new Line-of-Sight
Shade dial; the whole gameplay-latency surface was swept and certified on a
healthy machine; the launcher updates itself; and behind the curtain, all
85,150 skill × support pairs are now probed and ledgered as a standing
regression gate, so a gem that silently does nothing can never ship unnoticed
again.
