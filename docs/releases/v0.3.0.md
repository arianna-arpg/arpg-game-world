## Install

**Windows** — download `HollowWake-Setup-0.3.0.exe` below and run it. It is a
normal game installer: pick a folder (defaults to a per-user install, no admin
rights needed) and it puts a **Hollow Wake** shortcut on the desktop and in the
Start Menu, plus an uninstaller entry in *Apps & features*. Windows SmartScreen
may warn that the publisher is unknown — the build is unsigned — choose
**More info → Run anyway**.

**Linux / Steam Deck** — download `HollowWake-0.3.0-x86_64.AppImage`, mark it
executable (`chmod +x`), and run it. On the Deck, add it as a non-Steam game
from Desktop Mode (or re-run the install one-liner from STEAM.md — it fetches
the latest release). Launching from Steam boots straight into the game.

Saves live in your per-user data folder (Windows: `%APPDATA%/Hollow Wake`), so
installing, updating, or uninstalling the app never touches them. The launcher
checks GitHub on start and tells you when a newer release exists — its Update
button opens the download page.

## Highlights since v0.2.1 (308 commits in 8 days)

**The map became a world of countries.** A heaven and a hell bracket the run
now: THE AETHERIAL — cloud shelves above the sky with the High Spires, the
wind-swept Driftways, and the Ascent event that launches you up to them — and
THE CAUL, a Giger-grown biome where the terrain itself fights back. Between
them: the desert rebuilt as a three-face country (dunefields, glasspans, the
Swelter's daylight tax, mirages, the Sirocco Court and its buried lost
places); the jungle Thicket with swallowed Sunken Ruins; the haunted Gloamwood
and its Long Night; Deepwinter's marching frost front and the Rimebound
winter court; the Karst stone country and the Littoral coast; the Sepulcher
Sands entombed under the deep desert; the crystal country that answers your
blows; the Flesh — the war-wound grown into a living country of meat; and
cavern strata stacked beneath everything, because the world now knows how
deep it goes. Pits are doors: walk off an edge and there is a below.

**Sovereigns rose to rule them.** Roaming Primeval world bosses; the Iron
Bell, a walking mausoleum tolling through the karst; the Winter King enthroned
at the Glacial Heart — an ice arena ringed by a chasm, grinding shear-discs
and a sweeping flail, where his own knockback insurance can be broken and the
winter hurled into its own deep; a ghost ship, the Wraithsail, wandering the
open ocean; the War Below, an eternal eight-lord struggle for the underworld;
and courts everywhere — Drowned, Night, Sirocco, Starfall, the Sand
Sarcophate dynasty, the queenless Chitin Seethe whose murmurations take the
sky as living flocks.

**The ground came alive.** Living fog banks roam and conceal; the creep — a
living membrane — learned to march as advancing fronts, tidal walls that
always part around one safe corridor, and blood-pulse crests whose wakes dry
back to nothing; moving blade-tracks carve grooves and telegraph their
arrival; the Gloaming brings a dark you survive by holding light; ground
dissolves behind you on the causeway crossings; time itself became a dial
(stasis, menu freezes, chrono casts); weather fronts arrive as weather instead
of light switches, and roofs, caves and cellars genuinely shelter you from
the sky.

**Combat grew texture.** Defenses are now authored identity, not ambience:
poise, insight and energy shield start empty and are built — a wall of a
monster braces behind a true break-bar, a duelist reads your casts, and the
break windows are yours to open. Ground has a price and feet weigh it: enemies
route around lava, prefer their own roads, and dodge what's about to hit
them. Perpetual kiters tire. Enemy cognition got drives, feints, cast-reading
and punishes; walls blind perception and eat shots along one honest raycast;
and everything — doodads, projectiles, worm segments, moving blades — collides
exactly as drawn.

**Twenty-seven classes, three per star point, every kit at parity.** Plus the
Symbiote Arts, the Empyrean kata, the Gale family's wind-made roads,
timed Iai draws, chambered casting, ammunition magazines and fount sips,
conduit pumps, dice-as-trigger fortune skills, taunts, guard-bash, and an
unarmed floor so an empty slot still answers. Flasks are never locked out —
the reflex lane opens through your own casts.

**Wealth got lanes.** A lean loot baseline over a wide affix gamut (local and
global scopes), a salvage economy feeding an essence-gated Vault, vestige
socketables and Epitaph words, choice nodes and parallel passive
constellations (realms, grafts, boons), the wakeflame votive economy, and
tame-a-beast bonds with a Tracker's book that remembers your kills
account-wide.

**Things to do besides kill.** Zone objectives as a real vocabulary — survey
spires, bounty writs, offerings, attunement circuits, loseable processions,
defend-the-village boroughs, extraction stands — plus vendetta writs where the
world answers your own deeds, and a world that stays wakeful: worldstate
persists across saves, so the war you left keeps its lines.

**It runs better than ever.** Runtime ground changes no longer stall the
frame (async texture uploads); render caches release their memory on zone
swaps instead of ratcheting forever (the Firefox slow-creep is fixed); the
perception system stopped stampeding; and three headless harnesses — balance,
generation QA, and a real-window performance gate over every biome — now
stand behind every change.

**Playability.** One drag-and-drop grammar everywhere, a controller aim pass,
a UI scale dial for every reading surface, camera modes, an interactive
overworld map, a low-life heartbeat vignette, and a project website with a
searchable database.
