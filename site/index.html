Infinite by design

A world that generates itself, and never stops

Two hand-built anchors — the town of Lastlight and Wayfarer's Crossroads — open onto a frontier with no edge. Step through a frontier portal and a fresh zone is minted and stitched onto the map, one level deeper. It grows as far as you're willing to walk.

The map grows as you explore

Frontier portals mint a brand-new zone on the way through and graft it into the world graph. There's no end of content — the graph itself is the difficulty curve, every step deeper raising the stakes.

Descend into the Underworld

The frontier doesn't only spread — it goes down. Delve deep enough and you tear open the Hellgate: hell steppes, volcanic cauldrons, and the River of Flame winding to a guaranteed Hellforge.

Every zone asks something different

Clear the packs, survive the waves, escape a zone that never goes quiet, destroy the spawners, or hunt down the boss. Objectives vary the play, so no two runs feel like the same checklist.

The lay of the land

Dozens of lands, one growing frontier

The world is stitched from a deep roster of biomes — verdant to infernal — each with its own terrain, set-pieces, and monster ecology that shifts by depth. Here's a handful; shuffle for more, and hover any card for the full read.

A card marked Landmark — the Ossuary, the Grand Arena, the rifts — is a set-piece reached through a specific event, not common terrain you roll into.

The world runs without you

World-events, each its own mini-campaign

Optional overlays erupt across the map on their own clocks — war-hosts, plagues, rifts, hunts, invasions. Chase them, dodge them, or get caught inside. Shuffle the deck, and hover a card for what wakes it and what it fields.

Each is a self-contained package layered onto the world — the mix you meet is tuned per run from the ones you've unlocked.

A breathing world

Factions fight over ground you don't own

Territory is a living field. 10+ factions gain and lose ground based on which of their monsters are alive, and go to war with each other — a zone you knew as undead can flip to goblin colours while you're away. The clock and the weather change who's out there.

Territory that shifts

Zones become owned, contested — where two hostile factions both spawn and tear into each other — or conquered, flipping the whole population. You're the lever: thin out the goblins here and goblin power falls.

Day, dusk, night, dawn

A full day/night wheel turns as you play — and it's load-bearing. The Haunting only binds after dark, and dawn unbinds it. What hunts you depends on the hour.

Weather with teeth

Rain, storm, fog, ashfall, snow — and the Blood Moon, under which the undead walk twice as thick. Every front re-weights who spawns where.

The sea is a place

Cast off from a dock and you are the boat — the coastline streams in, and every shore you nose up to lands a real, generated zone, with one-off voyage islands riding on top.

Your contract with the dark

Death means something — you decide how much

Run Mortal and a fall ends the character, pays the essence tithe, and leaves a corpse any successor can reclaim. Swear the Immortal covenant and death can't keep you — the world stops counting. Hire fallen heroes, or bring a friend.

Corpse runs

Your death drops a reclaimable corpse holding your equipped gear, marked on the world map — the skill and support gems you carried are lost to the fall. The road back is part of the danger.

Mercenaries

Hire archetype blades at the outpost — the archer, the desert warrior, the caster-for-coin — synthesized fresh at your own power. Retired heroes become veterans.

Early co-op

Play together, host-authoritative; enemies scale with the party so solo stays untouched. Early support, still being hardened — but the seats are there.

Make it yours

Skills are loot. Everything is a lever.

You don't unlock skills on a class tree — you find them. Active skills and supports drop as gems, slot into any build, and recombine into your own way to play. Hover anything in the rack to see the real numbers.

Run from source

Requires Node.js and npm. Then:
# first-time setup
npm install

# browser dev mode → localhost:5173
npm run dev

# the desktop app (Electron)
npm run game

# type-check + production build
npm run build
Controls: WASD to move, LMB/RMB + 1–6 for skills, C / B / P / M for character, skills, tree and map — all rebindable.
Early prototype

Hollow Wake is in active development. The systems are broad and working — skills, supports, items, passives, procedural worlds, AI, factions, co-op and permadeath — but the presentation is intentionally minimal for now: art is deliberate placeholder geometry rendered through the shading layer, and audio isn't in yet. Expect balance, content and class identity to keep shifting between versions.
